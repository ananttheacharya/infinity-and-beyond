Mission Overview

Physics-Informed Digital Twin (PIDT) for Turbojet Health Monitoring

The Objective

Build a competition-winning, physics-informed, interpretable digital twin for a four-stage single-spool turbojet engine. Avoid purely data-driven "black-box" models. Embed first-principles thermodynamics.

Evaluation Criteria

  • 30% Health Estimation Accuracy
  • 25% Optimization Quality / Surrogate Perf.
  • 20% Engineering Justification
  • 15% Innovation & Physics Consistency
  • 10% Dashboard & Interpretability

Why Our Plan Wins

Most teams will optimize purely for prediction accuracy (MSE) and fail the "Engineering Justification" and "Physics Consistency" criteria. We will win by:

  1. Offloading Physics: Ensuring inputs to the ML models are physically meaningful (thermodynamic invariants).
  2. Causal Explanations: A rule-base ensuring the output is interpretable to HAL engineers.
  3. Interactive UX: A Mission Replay Engine and Causal Failure Graph.

Methodology Pathway

Physics derivations and feature engineering strategies

Compressor Thermodynamics

The health of the compressor is heavily influenced by fouling and erosion, which manifest as drops in the pressure ratio and isentropic efficiency.

  • Pressure Ratio (PR_comp): P2 / Pamb
  • Temperature Ratio: T2 / Tamb
  • Isentropic Efficiency Proxy: Derived from (P2/Pamb)^((γ-1)/γ) compared to the actual T2/Tamb rise. A decline in this value is a direct indicator of fouling.

Combustor & Turbine Diagnostics

  • Combustor Pressure Loss: (P2 - P3) / P2. Rises with liner degradation.
  • Turbine Temperature Drop: T3 - T4. A falling drop for a given pressure ratio signals turbine erosion or tip clearance loss.
  • Normalized Shaft Speed: RPM / √(Tamb). Corrects for aerodynamic similarity, allowing valid comparisons across different ambient conditions.

Hybrid Modeling (PINN)

Instead of feeding raw sensors into a black-box model, our Neural Network takes these explicitly calculated thermodynamic ratios as inputs. The loss function is constrained by mass and energy balance equations to prevent non-physical predictions, ensuring high "Physics Consistency" scores.
